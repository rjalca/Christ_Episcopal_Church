In November 1903, All Saint's Church was started in the University District of Seattle, Washington. The church was renamed Christ Episcopal Church in its infancy. The first Chapel was constructed in 1904 and the first church in 1909 on the corner of 47th Street and Brooklyn Avenue.

In 1914, the original church was moved to the lot south of the corner and remodeled to serve as a parish hall. A new church was built on the corner lot.

In 1931 the parish hall was moved and given to the YMCA for use as a gymnasium. The actual church itself was moved to the south lot, and the corner lot was leased to Shell Corporation for a gas station.

In 1954, the new church house was dedicated on November 21. This building was to be for offices, Sunday School classes and meeting rooms.
